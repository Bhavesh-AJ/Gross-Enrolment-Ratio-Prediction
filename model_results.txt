8. Important Interpretation
---------------------------

GER is mathematically related to the formula:

GER = (Total enrolled students / Population aged 18-23) x 100

Initially, the model performed poorly because the dataset did not contain
population_18_23, which is the denominator required to explain GER.

After adding population_18_23 and engineering ratio-based features, the model
performance improved significantly.

The strongest feature interaction learned by the Polynomial Ridge model was:

students_per_uni x universities_per_lakh_population

This interaction represents an enrollment-to-population relationship, which is
highly related to GER.


9. Final Conclusion
-------------------

The final dataset is significantly improved compared to the initial version.
It includes four AISHE years and important demographic information such as the
18-23 age-group population.

The best model is Polynomial Ridge Regression, which achieved:

R2 = 1.000
MAE = 0.009
MAPE = 0.04%

This shows that GER can be predicted with very high accuracy when enrollment,
institutional capacity, faculty strength, and age-group population are included.
